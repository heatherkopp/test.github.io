<p> I am Heather M. Kopp, an assistant professor in the <a href="https://www.depts.ttu.edu/politicalscience/" target="_blank">Department of Political Science</a> at <a href="https://www.ttu.edu/" target="_blank">Texas Tech University</a>. My research focuses on the domestic determinants and consequences of interstate conflict and on political competition. Broadly, I have research interests in interstate and intrastate conflict processes, violent nonstate actors, voting behavior, public opinion and interstate conflict, and party competition over foreign policy issue ownership. I have work published in <em>Political Research Quarterly</em>, and I teach graduate and undergraduate international relations courses. </p>
  
<p> I earned my Ph.D. in Political Science from the <a href="https://www.missouri.edu/" target="_blank">University of Missouri</a> in 2024 and my B.S. in Political Science and History from <a href="https://www.truman.edu/" target="_blank">Truman State University</a> in 2019. </p>
